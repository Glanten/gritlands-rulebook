Counselling

Everypony

The Gritlands aren't just physically threatening and demanding. They take their toll on the mind as well. Those with the empathy and strength to support others are essential in driving society forward in the face of adversity. Without these counsellors, nihilism and despair would all too easily quench human (and near-human) spirit.

Counselling is a healing skill that allows your patient to ignore the negative effects of psych-type Consequences. It does not remove Consequences (use Psychoanalysis for that) but it can act as a quick fix when you need a fellow troubleshooter to get back on their feet and stop jumping at shadows in the middle of a HOTZONE.

Level 1
  • You may spend 1 GRIT and engage in 2 minutes of appropriate roleplaying to treat a Minor (Psyche) Consequence. After treatment, the recipient can ignore the negative effects of the Consequence (although the consequence slot remains filled). There is no GRIT cost for this skill the first time you use it on a given day.
  • You may spend 2 GRIT and engage in 2 minutes of appropriate roleplaying to treat a Moderate (Psyche) Consequence. After treatment, the recipient can ignore the negative effects of the Consequence for 30 minutes (although the consequence slot remains filled).
  • You may spend 3 GRIT and engage in 2 minutes of appropriate roleplaying to treat a Major (Psyche) Consequence. After treatment, the recipient can ignore the negative effects of the Consequence for 30 minutes (although the consequence slot remains filled; the Consequence is not stabilised and will continue to worsen towards a Critical Consequence).
  • You may not use Counselling to treat your own Consequences.
Level 2
  • As level 1 plus:
  • You gain an additional free daily use of this skill to treat Minor (Psyche) Consequences.
  • You may spend 2 GRIT and engage in 2 minutes of appropriate roleplaying to treat a Minor (Psyche) Consequence you have suffered, in the same way as you would treat another character. You cannot employ one of your free daily uses of Counselling when treating yourself (i.e. treating yourself always costs GRIT).
Level 3
  • As level 2 plus:
  • You gain yet another additional free daily use of this skill to treat Minor (Psyche) Consequences.
  • You gain a free daily use of this skill to treat Moderate (Psyche) Consequences.
  • You may spend 3 GRIT and engage in 2 minutes of appropriate roleplaying to treat a Moderate (Psyche) Consequence you have suffered, in the same way as you would treat another character. You cannot employ one of your free daily uses of Counselling when treating yourself (i.e. treating yourself always costs GRIT).

Counselling Talents

Take your own Advice (1 skill point)

Requires Counselling at level 2.

You gain one free daily use of this skill to treat a Minor (Psyche) Consequence you have suffered (as above).

Popping Pills (1 skill point)

Requires First Aid at level 1.

If you employ a first aid kit physrep as a focus, you gain an additional free daily use of this skill to treat a Minor (Psyche) Consequence (as above).

Chill Pill (1 skill point)

Requires First Aid at level 1 and Counselling at level 2.

If you employ a first aid kit physrep as a focus, you gain an additional free daily use of this skill to treat a Moderate (Psyche) Consequence (as above).

Hey! It's me, it's me! (1 skill point)

If the character you are treating with this skill is a member of your group, it costs 1 less GRIT to treat them (to a minimum of 0).

Ask for Help (1 skill point)

Requires Counselling at level 2.

If you can speak with a member of your group as part of your appropriate roleplaying, you can reduce the cost of treating your own Minor (Psyche) Consequence by 1 GRIT.

Self Help Group (1 skill point)

Each level of Counselling allows you to simultaneously treat 1 extra player, increasing the treatment time by 1 minute for each patient beyond the first, but with no additional GRIT cost. If you have the "Ask for Help" talent, you may also treat yourself in this manner (note that this is the cheapest way to treat your own Psyche Consequences!). All patients in the group (including yourself if appropriate) must have suffered their Consequences from the same source.

When determining whether the source of a group's Consequences is the same, consider the following questions: were the Consequences gained during the same encounter? Are they from the same environmental hazard? Has everyone developed the same (or similar) phobia/s? Are they presenting the same symptoms and behavioural quirks? Do they all have the same type of referee-issued TAG?

Practised Patter (3 points)

Requires the Counselling skill at level 3.

Three times per day, you may invoke your hard-earned experience as a counsellor as part of your appropriate roleplaying for this skill. If you do so, reduce the GRIT expenditure for this treatment by 1 (to a minimum of 1 GRIT).
