D-Fens

Everypony

Stopping people from pushing sharp, dangerous objects through you is an essential ability. We recommend the barrier method.

Level 1

Once per day you can resist a weapon call (the player / NPC making the call doesn't spend GRIT but cannot repeat the call for 5 seconds).

Level 2

Twice per day you can resist a weapon call (the player / NPC making the call doesn't spend GRIT but cannot repeat the call for 5 seconds).

Level 3

Thrice per day you can resist a weapon call (the player / NPC making the call doesn't spend GRIT but cannot repeat the call for 5 seconds).

D-Fens Talents

'Eavy Metal (1 skill point)

You can wear heavy armour (i.e. 'Eavy Metal). It provides 2 additional GRIT (cumulative with War Paint).

Shield (1 skill point)

You can use a shield.

I am Iron Man (1 skill point)

You must be a Warrior or an Engineer.

If you are wearing 'Eavy Metal, it provides 1 additional GRIT.

Bulletproof (1 skill point)

You must be a Warrior.

If you are wearing 'Eavy Metal, you may spend 1 GRIT to avoid Grievous Harm from a firearm as if it was a melee weapon or bow/crossbow hit by taking a strike-down effect. Each level of D-Fens gives you one use of this ability per day.

Design Notes

Resistance to calls

We almost didn't include these abilities, because having your jam trump my jam isn't much fun. Gritlands isn't intended to be a PVP game; this skill means you might need to think about when and how you deploy your weapon calls if you get into a scrap with another player (as you do not spend the GRIT if the effect is not taken).

What we absolutely will not do is routinely give resistance skills to the general opposition. Instead, we might give a limited subset of the opposition limited resistances where it makes narrative sense (if it's using its claws and horns to gouge you, you can't disarm it. If it is a 2 ton automated defence turret you can't strike it down, etc.)
