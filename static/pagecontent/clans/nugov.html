Nugov

Who are NuGov?

The remnants of national civil power centres from the Before Times, and those who worked with or supported them closely, clinging onto the traditions of the past as a means of unlocking the secrets of the Gritlands. These are the descendants of government enclaves desperate to turn the clock back to a time when they had power, respect, and stability.

Three Things They Are

  • Desperately harking back to a past that didn't actually exist. Imagine a tourist's idea of England writ large, plasticy and twee.
  • Hierarchical. People know their place and don't question it, this way is the best way to rebuild the new world irrespective of the cost in personal freedom. Everyone in the system knows their place and is happy with it.
  • A pastiche of 80's Conservatism. Authoritarianism dressed up as opportunity.

Three Things They Are Not

  • Nazis. NuGov does not discriminate against race, sexuality, gender, or religious beliefs.
  • Soldiers. The military establishment was washed away in the End Times, NuGov are very much the remains of civil authority, not military authority. As such, overly militaristic tropes and costume are not appropriate.
  • Self-aware. The people of NuGov are not self aware of the utter ludicrosity they are perpetuating as they risk life, limb and resource to recover a near pristine red phone box from a hellish rad zone infested with kill-bots.

Look and Feel

Battered 80s business attire, bowler hats, old police/fire/ambulance/nurse uniforms, riot armour, overalls, number stencilled jumpsuits for the lower echelons of society, briefcases, elaborate tea ceremonies, Thatcher Icons, typical cockney attire (pearly Queens and Kings), awful UK-centric tourist tat, truncheons, fire axes, hammers, stanley knives. Rigid hierarchy, greed. A mixture of upright corporate types, shifty cockneys, and indentured servants in numbered jumpsuits.

What To Avoid

  • Steampunks
  • Camo gear / military uniforms
  • Urban ninjas
  • Cyberpunks

Hierarchy

NuGov is kept running by a labyrinthine bureaucracy of ministers, secretaries, inspectors, overseers, underseers, directors, indirectors and at least a dozen even less defined roles all associated with a legion more subcommittees, portfolios and steering panels whose names and purpose have been lifted whole from documents from the Before without care or consideration for context or meaning. This pinstriped heap constitutes the Cabinet - and perched at its peak is the Minister of the Prime Chamber and it is by their biro that fortunes rise and fall - entire departments being formed and dissolved by the filing (or misfiling) of paperwork descending from the Chamber.

The Delegated Powers Legislation Committee is the current body with the authority to approve applications for the formation of C.R.A.S.S expeditions. Through its head Permanent Senior Civil Servant and under the watchful eye of the Prime Chamber's own Permanent Under-Secretary to the Minister with Portfolio, the DPLC is responsible for assigning representatives from the relevant ministries required for delving into a hot-zone. This includes, but is not limited to:

  • The Sub-Committee on Security and Investment in Technological Artefacts
  • The Abhuman Audit and Risk Committee
  • The Biological and Chemical Polyphenol Regulation Committee
  • The Secondary Board of Munitions
  • The Speaker's Committee on Electoral and Administrative Affairs

ID Cards

Citizens of the NuGov must, at all times, have their identity card prepared and ready to present to a ministry representative. ID cards are not necessarily uniform in appearance, but they must contain specific information:

  • Name (the individual's handle, moniker, or identifying designation)
  • Borough or Parish (the individual's current abode)
  • Tax Bracket (rated from A to 7, including sub-bracket rated from Î© to iiv)
  • Humanity (genetic adherence to baseline human biology, represented on a percentile scale)

Mutants and NuGov

NuGov has, since its earliest records, been vexed and perturbed by mutants: interpretations of its founding documents clearly state that every aspect of a "phenotypically challenged" individual's day to day life must be supported by additional paperwork and licences: from the "certificates of genetic stability" (form AG- TACA-0121) that need to be added to their internal passports (and regularly renewed), to the various addendums and handling forms needed to adapt standard paperwork for mutant use

This nightmare of additional bureaucracy means that any decent, pencil-pushing citizen worth their pinstripe suit would rather just not deal with mutants at all. Not out of hatred or prejudice - but because who wants to be caught short when it comes to paperwork?

This is an arrangement that most mutants seem fairly happy with.

Territory

The Capitol, also known as Fortress Keynes, is the remains of a perfectly-structured city that somehow avoided the Scars of the end times. Craters, sinkholes, and trench networks form an exceptionally hazardous moat around the city, limiting access to tunnels bristling with traps, dead ends, and patrols of highly-regulated enforcers.

Role Among the Clans

NuGov's strict adherence to rules and procedure has made them the perfect caretakers of the clans' water supply. Massive industrial pumps bring water up from aquifers deep below the surface to purification tanks. NuGov not only treats and tests the water, but also monitors the aquifers and limits their exploitation through measuring and rationing appropriately.

While every clan has solar stills and rainwater traps, NuGov can not only provide water in unrivalled quantities, but it can also guarantee their water is clean, safe, and good for your teeth.

Why Play NuGov?

You should play in NuGov if you have a keen sense of the satirical, a penchant for the highs and lows of 80s culture and either love or hate the Tories
