Tasks

Any activity that is governed by games mechanics (outside of Combat) is a TASK.

Some skills provide access to TASK that a character can choose to undertake in specific circumstances.

Other TASKS are detailed on the information TAGS found scattered throughout the game.

A TASK has an outcome that players (generally) want to achieve.

All TASKS require a period of appropriate roleplay as a minimum to achieve the outcome.

Most TASKS will also require additional prerequisites to be met. These could include one or more of the following:

  • GRIT Expenditure (one off, or over time) by an individual OR group of players
  • Possession of certain Skills, possibly at certain levels, by one or more of the participants
  • Possession of certain items or Information, by one or more of the participants
  • A successful TASK check (a randomising factor, consisting of a bag of beads, from which certain colours must be drawn)

The most complex TASKS will make use of a GERM (Gritlands Ersatz Referee Mechanism): This is a set of numbered cards, each of which contains directions for completing the TASK and lists choices the player may take. Each choice then directs them to a different numbered card. Cards may also contain additional information, or direct the player to do various other things.
