Tasks

Any activity that is governed by games mechanics (outside of Combat) is a TASK.

Some skills provide access to TASK that a character can choose to undertake in specific circumstances.

Other TASKS are detailed on the information TAGS found scattered throughout the game.

A TASK has an outcome that players (generally) want to achieve.

All TASKS require a period of appropriate roleplay as a minimum to achieve the outcome.

Most TASKS will also require additional prerequisites to be met. These could include one or more of the following:

  • GRIT Expenditure (one off, or over time) by an individual OR group of players
  • Possession of certain Skills, possibly at certain levels, by one or more of the participants
  • Possession of certain items or Information, by one or more of the participants
  • A successful TASK check (a randomising factor, consisting of a bag of beads, from which certain colours must be drawn)

The most complex TASKS will make use of a GERM (Gritlands Ersatz Referee Mechanism): This is a set of numbered cards, each of which contains directions for completing the TASK and lists choices the player may take. Each choice then directs them to a different numbered card. Cards may also contain additional information, or direct the player to do various other things.

if you are undertaking a TASK and are interrupted, the TASK fails and you lose the GRIT spent.

TASKS Details

In the HOTZONE there are various things to do. Let's call these things TASKS. That might be opening a door, or it might be repairing an air pump, or it might be investigating the decaying file system haunted by ancient system ghosts.

TASKS will generally be labelled and clearly described - Often with an associated TAG.

The action options and requirements will be clearly described. They will consist of one (usually at least 2) or more of the following things:

  • Appropriate Role-play. Standing around timidly tapping at a piece of armour is not appropriate role play for armour repair. Throw yourself into it - for scouting tags, once you have the information you should sniff the air, run the dirt between you r fingers, lick the wall, examine the lichen growing on the bark of a tree...
  • Time. Any task is going to take a period of time.
  • GRIT expenditure. A number of grit points must be expended. Sometimes a discount may be available for characters with certain skills.
  • Skill levels - certain skill levels are required.
  • Bead Pulls - some tasks require a character to draw beads from a bag. Once a certain number / colour / pattern has been drawn an outcome is achieved.
  • Doing a bead pull at Advantage means you draw 2 and choose the best one. A bead pull at disadvantage means you draw 2 (or more) and choose the worst one.
  • Gritlands Ersatz Referee Mechanism (GERMS) - a series of cards describes each step in the task resolution; some cards present choices and options for the card you go to next.
