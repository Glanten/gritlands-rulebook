Characters

Each player should have an active character with which to play the game.

Each Player can have only one active character at any time.

If a player's active character dies or is retired, the player may replace them with a new character.

No player may play a dead or retired character. Once a character has died or been retired, they are permanently removed from play.

Characters are defined by their base attributes (see below) which come from their choice of Clan, Archetype, Skills, and Talents.

Character Creation

Clan
A character's clan informs their appearance, philosophy, and roleplaying cues.

Archetype
Archetype determines a character's specialism and routes of progression.

GRIT
All characters start with a baseline of 6 GRIT.

GRIT is a pool of points which are used to absorb harm and fuel abilities.

Consequences
All characters start with a baseline of 4 Consequence slots.

Characters track harm that they do not absorb with GRIT as Consequences. An overabundance of Consequences can lead to character death (or worse).

Skills
Skills allow characters to interact with the world (and other characters) in powerful ways.

Most skills progress from level 1 to level 3.

Talents
Talents are abilities beyond skills and are normally specialisations within a skill that make a character even more powerful in a specific area.

Equipment
Some equipment will provide a mechanical advantage to its user; other equipment just makes a character look cool (surely the real goal of any LARP).
