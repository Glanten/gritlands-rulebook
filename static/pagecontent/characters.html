Characters

Each player should have an active character with which to play the game.

Each Player can have only one active character at any time.

If a player's active character dies or is retired, the player may replace them with a new character.

No player may play a dead or retired character. Once a character has died or been retired, they are permanently removed from play.

Characters are defined by their base attributes (see below) which come from their choice of Clan, Archetype, Skills, and Talents.

Character Creation

Intro

Characters consist of a concept, a clan, an archetype, GRIT, consequence track, skills and talents.

Concept

Characters should have a strong and setting appropriate concept. The majority of characters should be part of a group. Details regarding your character's name, attitudes and beliefs, ideals, goals, personality traits and more are what brings your character to life, rather than the mechanical skills and talents you select.

Clan

The Clan that a character belongs to will shape their outlook, agenda, approach, and flavour their mechanical options in terms of available archetypes and skills. Each of the clans provides a varying pool of centralised resources which are shared between the groups that make them up.

Grit

Every Character starts with a pool of 6 GRIT. This can be modified by Skills, Talents and Equipment.

Consequences

Every character starts with the following Consequence Track. This can be modified by Skills and Talents.

0 1 2 3 4 0
Healthy Minor Moderate Major Critical Terminal

Skills and Talents

Skills are broad or powerful mechanical abilities players can buy for their characters.

Skills are purchased in levels, with each level granting additional benefits. Each skill can be purchased up to Level 3.

In addition to the abilities a skill grants, it also gives access to a set of talents. These are cheaper, more limited abilities, which often improve on the base skill in minor ways.

Starting Skills and Choosing Skills

Every character starts with the following 'Everypony skills and talents':

  • & Chewing Gum skill at level 1
  • The Pipe Guns talent
  • The Hand Weapons talent
  • The War Paint talent

Every character that picks an Archetype to belong to gets the 3 Archetype Base Skills for their archetype, and 12 more points to spend on talents and skills. Any Character who eschews the archetypes is an Oddball. They get 18 points to spend on skills and talents.

Purchasing and Advancing Skills

For each archetype skills are divided into 3 categories:

Core skills: These are the skills most closely tied to the Archetype. They always include the Archetypes Unique skill(s). Core skills cost 3 points per level.

General skills: These are the skills which are more broadly developed by the archetype. General skills cost 4 points per level.

Uncommon skills: These skills are only distantly related to the Archetypes core activities, or they are very difficult to develop in general. Uncommon skills cost 5 points per level.

Most Archetypes have two or three skills that they cannot access. That's life.

Some Skills and talents might have prerequisites - usually a minimum level in a given skill, another talent, or occasionally another skill before they can be purchased. These will be noted in parenthesis next to the skill/ talent name.

Some skills and talents may be limited to specific characters - most often this will be by archetype. Occasionally it will be a limited number of purchases per level of associated skill. The details should be clear from the descriptions provided.

When Advancing a skill from a lower to a higher level, the mechanical effect of that skill is replaced by the description at the new level.

Skill points not spent at the end of character creation are retained.

Trappings

In addition to their costume (which may or may not count as War Paint, or 'Eavy Metal if they have those skills) characters can have the following STUFF:

  • Physreps representing the FOCI for any weapon they have the Talent to use
  • Physreps representing the FOCI for any Shield they can use
  • Physreps representing the FOCI required for Any Skill or Talent they have (eg: First Aid Kit, Medical Bag, Survey equipment)

Other items from the STUFF list that fits their clan/group and character concept.

Advancement

As your character survives their forays into the Hot Zone they might pick up a thing or two about staying not dead.

You earn 2 skill points at the end of each event you attend.

Update: You may re-spend your points at any time before an event. If you change archetype you lose your accumulated XP.
